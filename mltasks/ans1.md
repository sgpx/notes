# Scalars, units, and simple arithmetic review

**Question:** A drone flies for [[3]] minutes at a constant speed of [[12]] meters per second, and then flies for another [[1.5]] minutes at the same speed. 

1. Convert both times to seconds and compute the total distance traveled. Show your steps.  
2. Is speed a scalar or a vector? Briefly justify your answer.  
3. Can you add the drone’s distance traveled and its elapsed time to get a meaningful quantity? Explain why or why not in terms of units.

**Answer:**

for 3 minutes + 1.5 min the drone flies at a speed 12m/s

1) 3 min => 180 s, 1.5 min => 90s, total time = 270s, total distance = time x speed = 270*12 = 3240 meters
2) speed is a scalar as it just has a magnitude, velocity is a vector as it is has both magnitude and direction
3) distance and time cannot be added as they dont have the same dimensions [L] and [T]
